What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple numerical measure used to assess whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height. It is commonly used as a general health indicator, though it does not account for factors such as muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution.

BMI is a general guideline and does not differentiate between muscle and fat mass. It may not be accurate for athletes, older adults, or individuals with higher muscle mass. Always consult a healthcare professional for a comprehensive health assessment.
